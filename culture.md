# non code of conduct

We welcome everybody to join us in making this event a success. This is for everybody who is curious, intellectual, human, playful, serious, non-standard, creative. Pick any and add your own. This is not a festival, it is a community. 

As with any community, we need a few rules, but we don't invite rules lawyering. There is basically one rule: don't make us add a rule because of your behaviour. We will if needed, and we will name the rule after you.

It is important to remember that this is not a festival, but it is a celebration. People may get loud late in the night. But we will provide areas were the intent is to provide a quiet environment at night, but also keep it down a bit during the day. There will be small children who need their rest, and some of use older people may occasionally like that as well.

## For volunteers

You may be asked to volunteer at our event, that is part of being a community. Being a volunteer does not give you an elevated status, except in one area: we expect you to behave in a way that is an example to others. Be extra nice, even if someone appears rude to you. People may have had a short night, mosquitoes in their tents and you may speak to them before they've had their first coffee. If people are not responding well to your reasonable requests, do not argue with them, instead ask one of the appointed de-escalation volunteers to help out or take over.

## For the orga people

Work smart, not hard. The idea is that you have fun doing this and would want to do this again next year. Delegate tasks to the villages as much as possible. Don't overengineer, improvise, make do. Keep it cheap and cheerful.
